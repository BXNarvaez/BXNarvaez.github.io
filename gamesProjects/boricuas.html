Project "Boricuas"

Description

An assimilated Ignacia del Fuego goes on a journey to her motherland to reclaim her original cultural identity. In this experimental 2D platformer, use your lantern to reignite a dying flame inside of you, and reveal objects that were once hidden from your sight!

What I learned

Before it was a video game, the idea for Project "Boricuas" was an interactive art game. This idea got scrapped halfway through production for a number of reasons, which left me with only 5 months left. I decided to leverage the available resources I had in order to scout, hire, and fund a small team. This is how I found myself in the role of manager for a short time. Some important lessons I took from this project:

  • As a team manager, it's important to trust your teammates' abilities. There was an early fear of overworking the team and bombarding them with tasks. Once things started picking up I gave them a lot more to do. Upon doing so the feedback I received was not to worry so much. To them the work was just enough to handle.
  • Cultural representation in games is very important, but it alone is not grounds for an interesting game. The games industry is entering an era where any lack of diversity or inclusivity is simply unacceptable. I try my best to bring my culture into the work I do, and once I had a metaphor of revelation and ignition for decolonization and cultural preservation, it became very hard for me to see any other path for this project.
